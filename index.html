🎲 Monte Carlo Simulation (10,000 runs)
How to read this
The simulation runs the game 10,000 times using the projected score as the center point, with random variance applied to each team's output per run. A shared pace factor correlates both scores — if the game plays fast, both teams score more together.

Win % — How often Team A wins outright across all simulations. Above 50% = model favors A. This drives the moneyline verdict.
Cover % — How often Team A beats the market spread. This is the key number for spread bets — if it's near 50%, the market is sharp and there's no edge. Well above 50% = the spread has value.
Over % — How often the combined score exceeds the market total. Above 60% is a meaningful lean. Above 75% is a strong signal.
Avg Margin — The average winning margin across all simulations. Compare this to the market spread — the gap between them is your edge.
Histogram — The distribution of all 10,000 simulated margins. Green bars = Team A wins, red bars = Team B wins. The yellow dashed line is the market spread — everything to its right means A covers. The wider the distribution, the more variance in the matchup.

Injury / Adjustment Guide

Player Out — Use their PPG
20+ PPGStar / Go-to scorer → -4 to -5 pts 15-19 PPG2nd option → -3 to -4 pts 10-14 PPGStarter / role player → -2 to -3 pts 6-9 PPGRotation player → -1 to -1.5 pts Under 6 PPGBench → Don't adjust
Why not their full PPG?
The backup absorbs some production. Other players get more touches. The real loss is the efficiency gap between the star and his replacement — roughly 20-25% of the player's PPG.
Other Adjustments
Back-to-back games: -1 to -1.5 pts for fatigue
Momentum / hot streaks: Don't adjust. KenPom already captures team quality. Streaks are mostly noise.
